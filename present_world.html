Historical Retrospective Notice

You are viewing archived data from 2025 — as analysed from the vantage point of 2050. Many indicators shown here were at critical levels. Some were reversed; others were not. This archive exists so that future generations understand what was knowable — and what was ignored.

2025 vs 2050

Then and Now

2025 — Then 8.2B
World population. Urban migration accelerating. India surpassed China as most populous nation. Food security concerns in 34 countries.
2050 — Now 9.7B
Growth slowed to 0.4%/year. Vertical megacities house 72% of population. Synthetic food provides 40% of global calories, relieving farmland pressure.
2025 — Then +1.4°C
Average global temperature above pre-industrial baseline. Arctic ice loss at 40-year record. Insurance companies withdrawing from coastal markets.
2050 — Now +4°C
Paris Agreement targets missed. 340 million people relocated from flood zones. Carbon capture arrays partially offsetting ongoing emissions from legacy infrastructure.
2025 — Then 32%
Global electricity from renewables. Solar costs hit record lows. Fossil fuel lobbying blocking faster transition in major economies.
2050 — Now 87%
Fusion + solar + wind dominate. Last coal plant shuttered in 2043. Remaining 13% from legacy infrastructure in developing regions under rapid replacement.

Historical Record

Pivotal Moments of the 2020s

2025

AI Proliferation Threshold

Large language models reach mainstream global adoption. 800 million users within 3 years. Governments scramble to create regulation; most fail to keep pace with deployment.

2026

Arctic Ice-Free Summer

The Arctic Ocean records its first ice-free September. Scientists call it a "point of no return" moment. Methane release from permafrost accelerates past model predictions.

2027

Global Water Accord Signed

147 nations sign the Geneva Water Accord, establishing fresh water as a protected human right. First international framework with enforceable sanctions for aquifer over-extraction.

2028

First AGI Announcement

A US-based consortium announces verification of AGI. The declaration triggers emergency sessions at the UN and immediate market disruption across 40 industries.

2029

Amazon Sovereignty Act

Brazil, Colombia, and Peru sign the Amazonian Sovereignty Act — a binding agreement halting all new deforestation and establishing autonomous Indigenous-governed biopreserves.

State of the World — 2025

Key Sectors Snapshot

🏥

Healthcare

mRNA vaccines revolutionised. Cancer mortality down 28% since 2015. But 4.5B people still lack basic healthcare access.

🌾

Agriculture

Precision farming emerging. Vertical farms in 200+ cities. But 33% of agricultural land severely degraded.

🏙️

Urbanisation

56% of humanity in cities. Megacities of 10M+ proliferating. Urban heat islands worsening in tropical zones.

⚡

Energy

Solar cheapest energy source in history. Grid storage still bottleneck. Gas still 22% of electricity globally.

🚢

Transport

EV adoption at 28% of new car sales. Aviation still 2.5% of emissions. Shipping decarbonisation lagging badly.

📡

Communications

LEO satellite internet reaching remote areas. 5G in 85 countries. Quantum-encrypted communication networks in early deployment.

💰

Economics

Global GDP $110T but inequality at record highs. Automation displacing 14% of jobs annually. UBI pilots in 22 nations.

🎓

Education

AI tutors accessible to 1.2B students. But 258M children still out of school. Diploma credentialism beginning to erode.
